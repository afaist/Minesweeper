Individual taxes

There are N companies in a country. The laws of this country say that the tax of each company is decided by the President. The President wants to know which company pays the highest tax. However, none of the President's advisers know math well enough, so they asked for your help. Can you solve this problem?

The first line of the input contains the number of companies in this country.
The next N lines contain the annual income of each company. The numbers are non-negative integers.
The last N lines contain the taxes each company pays, that is, the percentage of the company's income.

All numbers are integers from 0 to 100.

Output the number of the company that pays the most taxes. Keep in mind that the enumeration of the companies starts from 1. If there are several companies with the same tax, output the number of the company with the lower number.

Examples:

Input (Ignore the text after the number, these are comments.):

2 // number of companies
50 // company 1 income
100 // company 2 income
10 // company 1 tax percentage
10 // company 2 tax percentage

Output:
2

Input:

3
100
50
50
10
20
10

Output:
1



Memory limit: 256 MB
Time limit: 8 seconds
